YAML - Open Gauquelin Database

YAML is a syntax which permits to express structured data. It is both convenient to write for a human (almost no " ' , ;) and readable by a computer program.
YAML is used to document the birth certificate of a person, through a file called .

Expressing structured information with YAML

The hierarchy of information is expressed with tabulations.
For example, this extract of a BC.yml file: 
person:
  birth:
    date: '1871-01-09 04:00'
    place:
      name: Saint-Affrique
      cy: FR
      c2: '12'
  sex: M
means that field person is conposed of 2 subfields: birth and sex.
Field birth is itself composed of subfields date and place.
